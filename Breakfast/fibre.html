What is fibre

Dietary fibre is found in wholegrain cereals and fruit and vegetables.

Fibre is made up of the indigestible parts or compounds of plants, which pass relatively unchanged through our stomach and intestines.

Fibre is mainly a carbohydrate. The main role of fibre is to keep the digestive system healthy.

Other terms for dietary fibre include 'bulk' and 'roughage', which can be misleading since some forms of fibre are water-soluble and aren't bulky or rough at all.

Problems due to lack of fibre

Research shows that most of the people are not getting enough dietary fibre. A diet low in fibre has been linked to:

  • constipation

  • haemorrhoids

  • diverticulitis.

  • irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)

  • overweight and obesity

  • heart disease

  • diabetes

  • bowel cancer

  • breast cancer

Food sources for fibre

There are various fodd resources that are rich in fibre content. Fibre foods are easily available and they can be included in the daily meal..

Some of them are:

  • wholewheat cereal biscuits

  • wholegrain bread

  • pieces of fruit (such as an apple and pear)

  • cup of frozen mixed vegetables

  • small, boiled potato (with skin on)

  • brown rice

  • wholemeal dry biscuits

  • almonds

  • leafy vegetables
